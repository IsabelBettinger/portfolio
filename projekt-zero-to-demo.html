Partner Project · CMS Integration

Zero to Demo

In four weeks, I led the delivery of two fully functional demo frontends for a CMS partner, built on top of our XP layer and presented at CUE Days 2025 to an audience of international media and publishing professionals.

Context

An XP layer for headless CMS

The product at the core of this project is an XP layer that sits between a headless CMS and the channels it delivers content to. It allows teams to manage and distribute content across multiple channels at once, while giving non-technical editors the ability to build and adjust pages without writing a single line of code.

This matters because most headless CMS setups require developer involvement for even minor layout changes. Our layer removes that dependency, putting page control back in the hands of the editorial team.

The goal of this project was to make that value tangible for a CMS partner, by building two complete demo frontends they could use to show prospective clients exactly what becomes possible.

The Challenge

Two production-ready demos, four weeks

Our CMS partner needed working demo pages to support their sales conversations. The demos had to showcase the full flexibility of our XP layer in combination with their system, be visually polished enough to present to enterprise prospects, and be ready in time for CUE Days 2025.

Four weeks is a very tight timeline for two complete frontends. The challenge was not just technical, it required clear prioritization, tight coordination with a small team across multiple roles, and fast decision-making to keep delivery on track without sacrificing quality.

My Approach

Focused delivery under a hard deadline

1

Scope Definition with the Partner

Worked closely with the CMS partner to define what the demos needed to show, which use cases mattered most for their sales conversations, and what "good enough to present" looked like within the time constraint.

2

Frontend Architecture & Design

Coordinated with the external designer and development team to establish the visual direction and component structure for both frontends, ensuring they worked seamlessly with our XP layer from the start.

3

Rapid Iteration & Integration

Led the team through an intensive build phase with short feedback cycles, continuously validating that the frontends reflected the product's capabilities accurately and that both demos were integration-ready in time for the event.

4

Presentation at CUE Days 2025

The finished demos were presented at CUE Days 2025, the annual global conference for the CUE community of media and publishing professionals, organized by Stibo DX.

Impact

Two enterprise leads generated at the event

The demos landed well at CUE Days 2025. Two enterprise prospects reached out directly following the presentation, having seen the demos in action. The project demonstrated not just what the product could do, but what was possible when partner integrations were built with clarity and intention.
