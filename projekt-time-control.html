Time Control

A scheduling feature that let editors and product managers automatically control when content elements appear and disappear, with no manual intervention required. It became one of the platform's strongest enterprise selling points.

The Challenge

Manual widget management was slowing teams down

For enterprise clients in media and publishing, managing time-sensitive content was a constant operational burden. Editors and product managers had to manually switch teasers, ads, and promotional elements on and off, often working outside office hours to hit publishing windows or campaign deadlines.

There was no way to schedule these changes in advance. Every update required a person to be present at exactly the right moment, which created bottlenecks, introduced human error, and made scaling content operations genuinely difficult. The bigger the client, the more painful the problem.

Teams were spending significant time and energy on a task that should have been automated, to prevent inefficiency and manual errors.

My Approach

From operational pain point to strategic product feature

I led the feature from initial discovery through to delivery, working directly with enterprise clients to understand exactly where the friction was and what a good solution needed to look like in practice.

1

Feature Definition

Defined the core capability: a scheduling layer that allowed any content element to be set to appear and disappear automatically at a specified date and time, with no manual action required at the moment of change.

2

Cross-functional Execution

Coordinated a team of developers and designers through agile sprint cycles, keeping the feature scope tight and the delivery timeline on track while maintaining close alignment with enterprise stakeholders.

3

Sales Enablement

Worked with the sales and marketing teams to position Time Control as a key differentiator in enterprise conversations, translating the technical capability into a clear business value story around saved time and reduced operational cost.

Impact

A new selling point that unlocked enterprise growth

Time Control had an impact that went well beyond the feature itself. For existing clients, it eliminated a recurring operational burden that had been quietly draining time and resources. For the sales team, it opened doors to a segment of enterprise prospects who had previously found the platform insufficient for their content workflows.

Time Saved

Eliminated the need for editors to be manually present at every content switch, freeing up significant time across teams.

Cost Reduced

Fewer manual touchpoints meant lower operational overhead for clients, a tangible cost saving they could point to directly.

Enterprise Unlocked

Became a primary differentiator in enterprise sales conversations, helping the platform win deals that previously weren't within reach.

The feature also demonstrated something broader: that understanding the operational realities of enterprise clients, not just their product preferences, is where the most meaningful opportunities often live.
