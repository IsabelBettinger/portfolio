Action View 2.0

Comprehensive redesign and relaunch of the platform’s core analysis feature, providing live Google SERP visibility data to media and publishing clients.

The Challenge

A critical tool with a broken experience

The existing Action View was one of the most business-critical features on the platform, but it was failing users badly. The interface was cluttered and the underlying data structure was deeply confusing, with no clear logical hierarchy to guide users through the information they needed.

But the visual chaos was only half the problem. Even users who managed to navigate the interface were left stranded: the data was there, but there was no guidance on what to do with it. Users could see numbers, but couldn’t tell what those numbers meant for their next move. The product was showing information without helping people act on it.

The core problem wasn’t the data, it was that users couldn’t see what the data was trying to tell them, or what they were supposed to do next.

My Approach

More than a redesign: adding intelligence to the product

I took full ownership of the problem and drove the project from initial discovery through to go-to-market. The scope went beyond fixing the interface. A core part of the work was introducing contextual action suggestions directly into the product, giving users clear, data-driven hints on what their next step should be based on what the SERP data was showing them.

Rather than jumping straight to solutions, I started by deeply understanding why users were struggling and where they were getting stuck after the data loaded.

1

Discovery & Research

Analyzed behavioral data to identify drop-off patterns, then ran targeted customer interviews to map specific pain points and unmet needs in the existing flow.

2

Concept, Design & New Features

Translated research findings into clear goals and validated concepts. Designed a cleaner interface in Figma alongside a new layer of contextual action suggestions, giving users in-product guidance on what to do next based on the data they were seeing. This transformed the tool from a passive data display into an active decision-support feature.

3

Agile Execution

Coordinated a cross-functional team across engineering and QA through agile sprint cycles, maintaining a tight feedback loop and unblocking dependencies as they arose.

4

Go-to-Market

Orchestrated a multi-channel rollout, coordinating marketing campaigns, user newsletters, and in-product onboarding to drive adoption of the redesigned feature.

Impact

Measurable results, immediate and lasting

The results were both immediate and sustained. By resolving the core usability issues and layering in contextual action suggestions, the product shifted from a source of frustration into a genuine decision-support tool. Users didn’t just understand the interface better, they knew what to do with it.

+88.2%
Surge in customer satisfaction scores for the Action View feature specifically
+34%
Uplift in overall platform engagement and usage behavior across all users

The improved experience had a cascading effect beyond the feature itself. A better core tool meant users spent more time in the platform, discovered more value, and reported higher overall satisfaction. The redesign became a key reference point in sales conversations with enterprise prospects.
